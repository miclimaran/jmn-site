Importing spare parts from Indonesia usually takes five steps: identify the exact part numbers you need, request a quote from an authorized distributor, agree on Incoterms and payment terms, receive export documentation, and arrange shipping to your destination port. Below is what each step actually involves.

## Why buyers source spare parts from Indonesia

Indonesia is a major manufacturing and assembly hub for Japanese automotive brands, including Suzuki, Toyota, Daihatsu, and Mitsubishi. This means genuine OEM parts are often available locally at lower cost than sourcing them from Japan directly, while still meeting the same factory specifications. For buyers in the Middle East, Africa, and Southeast Asia, Indonesia is also geographically closer, which shortens shipping times compared to importing from Japan or Europe.

## Step 1: Identify your part numbers

The fastest way to get an accurate quote is to provide the OEM part number directly. If you don't have it, the vehicle model, year, and a description or photo of the part are usually enough for a supplier to cross-reference it. Vague requests ("brake parts for a van") slow the process down and increase the risk of receiving the wrong item.

## Step 2: Request a quote

A serious supplier will ask for:

- Part numbers (or model/year if part numbers are unknown)
- Target quantities per part
- Destination port or airport
- Preferred Incoterms (see below)
- Required lead time

Sending this information upfront — rather than a single "do you have this part?" message — is what usually gets you a quote within one business day instead of several rounds of back-and-forth.

## Step 3: Understand the Incoterms

Incoterms define who is responsible for shipping, insurance, and customs at each stage of the journey. The three most common terms for spare parts export are:

- **FOB (Free on Board)** — the seller handles everything up to loading the goods onto the vessel at the origin port. The buyer arranges and pays for freight from there.
- **CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight)** — the seller also covers freight and insurance to the destination port, but the buyer still handles import customs clearance.
- **DAP (Delivered at Place)** — the seller delivers the goods to an agreed location in the buyer's country, before import duties are paid.

Choosing the right term depends on whether you already work with a freight forwarder in your own country or prefer the supplier to manage logistics end-to-end.

## Step 4: Review the Proforma Invoice and documentation

Before payment, you should receive a Proforma Invoice (PI) listing part numbers, quantities, unit price, total value, and agreed Incoterms. Once payment terms are confirmed, a legitimate exporter will also provide:

- Commercial Invoice
- Packing List
- Certificate of Origin (COO)
- HS Code classification for each item (see our [HS code guide](/blog/hs-code-guide-for-auto-parts-export) for more detail)

Missing documentation is one of the most common causes of customs delays, so confirm all of the above before the shipment leaves Indonesia.

## Step 5: Choose air or sea freight

Small or urgent orders typically move by air freight, while larger orders — especially full container loads (FCL) — move by sea. Less-than-container-load (LCL) sea freight is a middle option for medium-sized orders that don't justify a full container but are too large or heavy to ship economically by air.

## Step 6: Customs clearance at destination

Once the shipment arrives, your customs broker will use the HS codes and Certificate of Origin to calculate applicable duties. Working with a supplier who consistently provides accurate, complete documentation reduces the chance of your shipment being held for inspection.

## Working with an authorized distributor

Because genuine parts pass through official distribution channels, buying from an authorized distributor gives you traceability back to the manufacturer — useful if a warranty or quality dispute ever comes up. If you're sourcing Suzuki genuine parts specifically, confirm the supplier's authorized distributor status before ordering.
